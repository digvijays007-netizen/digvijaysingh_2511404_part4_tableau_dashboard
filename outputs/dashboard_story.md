# Dashboard Story - Part 4 Executive Dashboard

## Executive summary
The executive dashboard is designed to help leadership monitor sales performance, profitability, customer segments, category performance, shipping delay, discount impact, and return risk. Total sales are **217,017,651.92**, total profit is **33,306,312.84**, profit margin is **15.35%**, and return rate is **4.55%**.

## What is performing well
The strongest sales region is **South**, and **Technology** is the highest-profit category. These areas should be examined for practices that can be scaled.

## What is underperforming
The lowest-profit sub-category is **Office Supplies / Paper**. This area needs review for discounting, cost, return, or fulfilment issues.

## Visible risks
The dashboard highlights risk from returns, high discounting, and shipping delays. Discount decisions should be evaluated against profit margin, not only sales volume.

## Visible opportunities
Leadership can use region, category, segment, date, and ship mode filters to identify where profitable growth is strongest. The dashboard also supports targeted follow-up by isolating segments or categories.

## Recommended business actions
- Replicate practices from the strongest region and most profitable category.
- Review low-profit sub-categories for price and discount leakage.
- Monitor high-return categories and delay buckets.
- Use customer segment performance to improve campaign targeting.
- Add discount guardrails where profit falls as discount increases.

## Limitations of the dashboard
The dashboard is descriptive. It shows patterns and risks but does not prove causality. It should be paired with operational investigation and controlled tests before major policy changes.

## Suggested next analysis
- Margin decomposition by product and discount band.
- Carrier and fulfilment analysis for delayed orders.
- Return reason analysis by category and segment.
- Cohort analysis by campaign channel.
